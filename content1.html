WHAT IS COFFEE?

COFFEE?

C. ARABICA

The genus Coffea is in the family Rubiaceae in the major group Angiosperms (Flowering plants). Based on statistic, their are 104 total accepted species names from genus Coffea.







COFFEA ARABICA

Beferred to as gourmet coffee. These beans are said to have more delightful aroma and flavours. Arabica coffee flavors differ from which region they come from.

Descended from the original trees discovered in Ethiopia. These trees produce a fine, mild, aromatic coffee and represent approcimately 70% of the world's coffee production. These are flatter and more elongated, also has lower caffeine than Robusta.












COFFEA CANEPHORA

C. CANEPHORA

Robusta beans is said to contain more caffeine than Arabica. It tends to give out a more acidic and bitter taste. In the market, these beans are cheaper and is commonly used to make espresso.
